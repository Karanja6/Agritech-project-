Read below to know what to consider best according to your soil type , preference and recommended crop to plant.

  1. Alluvial Soil: Found near rivers and is fertile due to its rich mineral content. It's commonly used for crops like rice, wheat, and sugarcane.
  2. Black Soil: Also known as Regur soil, it is known for its high fertility and moisture retention capacity. It's suitable for cotton, cereals, and oilseeds.
  3. Red & Yellow Soil: These soils are well-drained and moderately fertile. They are suitable for crops like millets, pulses, and groundnuts.
  4. Laterite Soil: Found in tropical regions, these soils are rich in iron and aluminium. They are suitable for crops like cashew, tea, coffee, and rubber.
  5. Loamy Soil: A mix of sand, silt, and clay, it has good fertility and drainage. It supports a wide range of crops, including vegetables, fruits, and cereals.
  6. Sandy Soils: These soils drain quickly and are less fertile but are suitable for crops like peanuts, maize, and potatoes with proper irrigation.
  7. Black Cotton Soils: These are clayey soils that swell when wet and shrink when dry. They are good for cotton cultivation but require careful management.
